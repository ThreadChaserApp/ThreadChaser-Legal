3. Measurement Accuracy and Database Limitations

Thread matching results are computed by comparing user-entered dimensions against a reference database of nominal thread values. The accuracy of any result is therefore subject to:

  • The accuracy and calibration of the measuring instruments used by the user;
  • The accuracy of the values entered by the user;
  • The inherent tolerance bands of each thread standard, which the Website approximates but does not formally model in their entirety;
  • Database currency — thread standards are periodically revised and the Website's database may not reflect the most recent amendment to any given standard;
  • The simplified matching algorithm used, which compares major diameter and pitch only and does not evaluate all tolerance parameters (e.g. pitch diameter, minor diameter, surface finish, class of fit) defined by the relevant standard.
Always verify: A "match" indicated by ThreadChaser means only that your entered measurements fall within the configured tolerance percentage of a nominal standard value. It is not a certificate of conformance, and the matched standard may not be the correct one for your application.
